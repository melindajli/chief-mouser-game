# Playtest guide

Before adding more content, watch 2–3 people play the first ten minutes.
Say nothing. Take notes against this list.

## Setup

- Send them the live link cold — no explanation beyond "it's a game about
  Larry the Downing Street cat". How they orient IS the test.
- Desktop and phone at least once each (input schemes differ).
- Ask them to think aloud if they're comfortable; otherwise just watch.

## Watch for (first 10 minutes)

1. **The intro** — do they understand catching 2 mice is the goal at
   Battersea? Do they discover the charged pounce (hold SPACE / paw), or
   only ever tap-pounce?
2. **Scenes** — do they read the dialogue, or mash through it? (If they
   mash, the writing isn't the problem — the typewriter speed might be.)
3. **First task** — after the aide's first Red Box scene, do they know
   what to do and where?
4. **Navigation** — do they find the basement and first floor unprompted?
   Do they ever press M / the map button?
5. **The menus** — do they ever open Mischief or Honours without being
   told? (If not, the pause menu may need a nudge or badge.)
6. **Post Watch** — do they interact with the letterbox? (The guard's
   meeting scene hints at it.)
7. **Boredom point** — note the minute mark where they first look
   disengaged, and what they were doing.

## Ask afterwards (don't lead)

- "What was the goal of the game?"
- "What did the fish do?" (kipper economy legibility)
- "Was there anything you wanted to do but couldn't figure out?"
- "When would you play this — and would you send today's Sortie score
  to a group chat?"

## Known deliberate choices (don't fix on first complaint)

- No timers on career quests — only the Daily Sortie is timed.
- Walking pace is slow on purpose (cozy) — fast travel unlocks post-Garter.
- The music rests between passes; silence is intentional.
- Screen shake is off by default; there's a menu toggle.
